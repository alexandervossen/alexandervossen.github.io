Alexander Vossen

Assistant Professor of Entrepreneurship

University of Siegen

Welcome to my personal research page. I am a data-driven social scientist that uses computational linguistics to examine entrepreneurial strategy decisions. At its very core, entrepreneurship is a cross-sectional discipline, and my research perceives it at the intersection of strategy, marketing and organizational sociology literature. Most recently, I focus on how new ventures achieve optimal distinctiveness, i.e., how they handle the trade-off between simultaneously conforming to norms in order to appear legitimate and differentiating to appear novel and unique to realize competitive advantages. For handling this trade-off, I examine the role of cultural entrepreneurship, i.e., how entrepreneurs utilize cultural tools and elements, such as narratives or images, to convey information on their ventures and products. For empirical testing, I collect and analyze large scale secondary data from publicly available online sources and process them with artificial intelligence approaches, such as machine learning, image label recognition, and natural language processing.

Since 2016, I am an Assistant Professor of Entrepreneurship at University of Siegen. Prior to that, I held positions at RWTH Aachen University and BI Business School in Oslo, Norway. My work has been awarded several times and appears in international top journals such as the Journal of Business Venturing, International Journal of Innovation Management, and the Journal of Retailing. I am an ad-hoc reviewer for various international journals and serve on the editorial review board of Entrepreneurship Theory & Practice.
